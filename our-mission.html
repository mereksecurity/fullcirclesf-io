Our Mission

Restoring Land. Strengthening Community. Celebrating Culture.

The Philosophy

The Full Circle Vision

Our name is a statement of belief. In the natural world, everything moves in circles — seasons, water cycles, the relationships between plants, pollinators, and the soil beneath our feet. "Full Circle" reflects our conviction that healthy land, vibrant community, and living culture are not separate goals but one seamless whole.

When we restore a native meadow, we are also creating a gathering place. When we commission a public sculpture, we are also honoring ecological wisdom. When we bring neighbors together to plant seeds, we are planting something deeper — a shared sense of belonging to this extraordinary place called Santa Fe.

Pillar One

Ecological Consciousness

Santa Fe sits at 7,000 feet in the high Chihuahuan Desert — an ecosystem of remarkable resilience and extraordinary fragility. Decades of development, drought, and invasive species have degraded native habitats that took millennia to form. Full Circle works to reverse this damage, one planting site at a time.

We specialize in native plant restoration: chamisa, Apache plume, four-wing saltbush, desert willow, and dozens of other species that evolved here and belong here. Native plants use a fraction of the water demanded by conventional landscaping — a critical advantage in an era of deepening drought across the Southwest.

Our ecological work is guided by partnerships with the University of New Mexico, the New Mexico Native Plant Society, and local master gardeners who share our commitment to place-based conservation.

Pillar Two

The Santa Fe Difference

Santa Fe is unlike any other American city. Home to one of the world's great concentrations of working artists, a living Pueblo cultural tradition thousands of years deep, and a multicultural heritage woven from Indigenous, Spanish, Mexican, and Anglo threads — this place demands an approach to community work that honors its full complexity.

Full Circle never imposes a generic template on our community partnerships. We listen first. We ask: What matters here? Whose stories need to be told? What does this neighborhood need from its public spaces? The answers shape every project we undertake.

This commitment to genuine collaboration is what sets Full Circle apart — and why our projects earn lasting community ownership long after the planting is done and the scaffolding comes down.

Flagship Project

The Santa Fe River

The Santa Fe River was once a vital riparian corridor — a ribbon of life threading through the high desert, sustaining cottonwood bosques, birds, amphibians, and the communities that settled along its banks. Today it runs dry for most of the year, its banks eroded, its native vegetation largely gone.

Our multi-year River Restoration Initiative is rebuilding this living corridor stretch by stretch. We work in concert with the City of Santa Fe, the Santa Fe Watershed Association, and dozens of volunteer stewards to reestablish native riparian species and stabilize degraded banks.

Phase 2 is now complete. We are currently raising funds for Phase 3, which will extend our work to the upper watershed and install the first of several public art pieces celebrating the river's history and ecological significance.

How We Work

Our Approach

🌱

Restore

We assess each site's ecological history and design restoration plans grounded in native plant science, traditional land knowledge, and on-the-ground community input.

🛡️

Preserve

Restoration is just the beginning. We train community stewards, establish long-term monitoring programs, and work with city agencies to ensure lasting protection of every site we touch.

🔗

Connect

We weave restored landscapes into the fabric of community life — through public art, educational programming, neighborhood events, and partnerships with schools and cultural institutions.
